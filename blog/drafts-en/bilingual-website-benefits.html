The Business Value of Bilingual Websites: Why You Need Both Chinese and English

Travis Chen 2026-05-01 7 min read

Many Taiwanese businesses share a common blind spot: "All my customers are in Taiwan, so a Chinese website is enough."

But have you considered what happens when an overseas buyer searches for your product and lands on a Chinese-only website? They close the tab and move on to the next vendor. Even if your product quality is better and your pricing more competitive, the language barrier shuts potential customers out.

In this article, we discuss why bilingual websites are no longer exclusive to large corporations, and how HEY!BOSS uses AI to make bilingual website building simple.

1. Three Business Benefits of a Bilingual Website

1. Open the Door to International Markets

Taiwan's domestic market is limited, and many businesses eventually need to go international. The first step isn't attending overseas trade shows or hiring foreign sales reps — it's making your website speak English.

There are 1.5 billion English speakers worldwide. When your website has an English version, it's like having a 24/7 international sales representative, ready to serve potential customers from anywhere in the world.

2. Boost Brand Professionalism

Whether you have overseas customers or not, a bilingual website itself communicates one message: this company has a global perspective. In the business landscape, companies with English websites are perceived as more professional and trustworthy.

3. Double Your SEO Opportunities

A bilingual website means you appear in both Chinese and English search results. In some niche markets, English keyword competition is actually lower than Chinese, making it easier to achieve good rankings. It's like competing in two markets with one website.

2. Pain Points of Traditional Bilingual Websites

If bilingual websites are so valuable, why don't more businesses have one? Because the traditional approach is genuinely painful:

  • High translation costs — Professional translation for an entire website can cost thousands of dollars
  • Difficult maintenance — Chinese gets updated, English gets forgotten, and the two versions gradually drift apart
  • Double development costs — Two sets of pages, two URL structures, twice the testing and maintenance work
  • Inconsistent quality — Cheap translations read poorly; quality translations take too long

These challenges make many small and medium businesses shy away. But AI has changed the game entirely.

5. Which Industries Need Bilingual Websites Most?

Theoretically, all businesses benefit from bilingual websites, but these industries benefit especially:

  1. Export Manufacturing — Selling products overseas makes an English website a baseline requirement
  2. Tourism & Hospitality — Hotels, restaurants, and travel agencies serving international travelers who search in English
  3. Tech Startups — Investors and partners are global; an English website is the first impression of internationalization
  4. Professional Services — Accounting, legal, and consulting firms serving expat and multinational clients
  5. Education & Training — Attracting international students or overseas diaspora markets
