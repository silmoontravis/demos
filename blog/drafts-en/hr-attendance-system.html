HR System: Attendance, Leave, and Payroll All in One

Travis Chen 2026-05-05 7 min read

At the end of every month, is your HR specialist doing this: opening Excel, manually calculating tardiness, early departures, overtime, and leave from attendance records... then opening another Excel to calculate payroll? For a 50-person company, payroll calculation alone takes 2-3 days.

What's worse is when there's a mistake — employees complain and labor inspections result in fines. The pressure of manual payroll calculation is something only HR professionals truly understand.

An HR system exists precisely to solve these problems.

1. Attendance Management: Leaving the Punch Clock Behind

Traditional fingerprint punch clocks have several issues: you can only clock in at the office (what about field workers?), if the machine breaks there's no record, and data export formats are inconsistent.

Modern HR system attendance management is completely different:

  • Multiple clock-in methods — Mobile GPS clock-in, web-based clock-in, NFC tap — choose based on work type
  • GPS geofencing — Set a valid clock-in radius, such as within 100 meters of the office
  • Flexible scheduling support — Core hours, flexible start/end times, shift-based scheduling — different departments can have different attendance rules
  • Automatic calculation — Tardiness, early departures, and overtime hours are all calculated automatically, no manual reconciliation needed

2. Leave Management: Online Approval with Instant Notifications

Problems with paper leave forms: the manager isn't at their desk to sign, forms get lost, and leave balance calculations go wrong. An online leave system solves all of these at once:

  • Online requests — Employees submit leave requests via phone or computer, selecting the leave type, time period, and reason
  • Automated approval workflow — After submission, the direct supervisor is automatically notified and can approve or reject from their phone
  • Automatic leave balance tracking — How many annual leave days total, how many remaining, how many personal days used — employees can see it all themselves
  • Automatic annual leave calculation — Based on labor law regulations, annual leave days are automatically calculated from the hire date, no manual lookup required
  • Delegate assignment — When on leave, the system automatically notifies the designated delegate so work continues seamlessly

3. Payroll Calculation: The Most Error-Sensitive Process

Payroll calculation is HR's most critical and sensitive task. Pay too little and employees are unhappy; pay too much and the company loses money. Not to mention the various calculation rules for labor insurance, health insurance, and income tax withholding.

The HR system's payroll module helps you:

  • Auto-import attendance data — Attendance records feed directly into payroll calculations, no manual entry needed
  • Automatic overtime calculation — Weekday overtime at 1.34x/1.67x, rest day at 1.34x/1.67x, public holidays at 2x — the system determines and calculates automatically
  • Insurance deductions — Automatically calculates labor insurance, health insurance, and retirement fund contributions based on salary brackets
  • Tax withholding — Automatically calculates the appropriate tax withholding amount based on tax tables
  • Pay slip generation — Automatically generates electronic pay slips that employees can view within the system

4. RuoYi RBAC: Department-Level Permission Management

Permission management in HR systems is especially critical because payroll data is highly sensitive. Who can see whose salary? Managers can see their subordinates' data but not other departments'. The CEO can see everyone's data, but outsourced IT cannot.

  • Role definitions — CEO, department managers, HR specialists, regular employees — each role sees different data and functions
  • Data permissions — Department managers can only see their own department's data, no additional configuration needed
  • Operation permissions — Granular down to the button level; for example, HR can "edit payroll" but managers can only "view attendance"

5. Key Considerations When Implementing an HR System

The most common pitfalls when implementing an HR system:

  1. Clarify your company's attendance rules first — Many companies can't clearly articulate their own attendance rules (just how flexible is "flexible hours"?). Before systematizing, you need to define the rules
  2. Historical data migration — Employee tenure, remaining annual leave, accumulated overtime hours — all this historical data needs to be correctly imported into the new system
  3. Employee training — No matter how good the system is, it's useless if employees can't use it. Especially older employees may need patient guidance
  4. Parallel operation period — We recommend running old and new systems in parallel for 1-2 months to confirm the new system calculates correctly before fully switching over

Conclusion

HR operations are the foundation of business management. Attendance, leave, and payroll — get them right and nobody notices; get them wrong and everyone complains.
